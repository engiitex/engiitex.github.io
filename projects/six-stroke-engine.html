The Adjustable Cycle Engine Project - ACE

Group Members

Bethany Evans, Michael Kincaid, Joshua German, Natalia Gruszka, Mark Latocha, Sneha Ginododiya, LeJalen Huff, Fernando Cassiano Mohammed Alshawaf

Purpose

To design and build a vehicle powered by an internal combustion engine utilizing a 6-stroke cycle.

Goals Reached

Previous research done by graduate students have yielded simulations and analysis of a dual piston, instantaneously variable compression ratio engine design.

Short Term Goals

  • Designing CAD models an enclosure for the vehicle, including chassis, frame and bodywork.
  • Designing the engine itself, including new valvetrain ideas, engine block, compression ratios, cylinder configuration and general dimensions.
  • Designing a pneumatic storage, discharge and heat recovery system for the first exhaust stroke and second combustion stroke of the cycle.
  • Deliverables: Reports, Papers, Poster, Presentation, Prototype, Handoffs

Long Term Goals

  • Adopting and completing a valvetrain idea.
  • Running and finishing simulations for an optimized design for building or conversion.
  • Utilizing existing engine blocks for converting 4 stroke engines into working 6 stroke designs.
  • Building a ground-up working prototype of a 6-stroke engine using single cylinder and more complex cylinder configurations

Pitch

Our team is involved in researching the feasibility of building a six-stroke engine. It’s a new system that would allow for a car that runs on fresh air; that is environmentally friendly; is technologically advanced, and provides power and reliability with less air pollution.

Currently, we’re in the survey and research phase, and are looking to move to physical prototyping soon. If successful, we’d like to contract with a national automobile company that would adopt our technology into their cars.
