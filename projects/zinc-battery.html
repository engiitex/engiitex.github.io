The Zinc Battery Project

Basic idea for the production of the Zinc Battery

The logic for the cell is to grab the idea of a zinc air battery but turn it into a flow battery with powder zinc in a KOH solution and feed that zinc as the fuel into the car and at stations remove the zinc oxide that forms as a result of the reaction and feed new zinc in.

Achieved Goals

The following things have been achieved since the inception of this project:

  • The production of the car
  • The implementation of the circuitry of the car
  • The creation of the motor

The Remaining Goals of the Zing Battery Project

  • To create a "refuelable" electric vehicle with the simplicity and speed of conventional fuels.
  • To create a cell that can be replicated to produce enough power, 20 KWh.
  • To create a tank or array of cells that produces enough power and voltage to get the car to be able to travel 300 miles or less at the average speed of 60 mph.
  • To design the feeding system for the fuel.

Issue faced

The major issue delaying the Zinc battery over the past few semesters has been inability to make a working cell due to the lack of power or maximizing the chemistry of the elements.

Pitch

Our team is involved in researching the feasibility of creating a quick refueling capability for an electric vehicle. We’re looking to implement this by being the gasoline equivalent for electric vehicles. One reason for or choice of material is the ability to recycle it easily. Currently, we’re in the material research and prototyping phase, and we’re looking to move to the battery design phase soon. If successful, we’d like to contract with a national automobile company that would adopt our technology into their cars.
